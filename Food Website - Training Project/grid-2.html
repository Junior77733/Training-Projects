The Throwback Strawberry Cake That's Stealing the Show at My Cookouts

This poke cake's berry, berry good.

  • Ingredients

  • Baking spray with flour
  • 150 g (10 1/2 tablespoons) unsalted butter, softened
  • 400 g (14 ounces; 2 cups), plus 36 g (1 1/4 ounces; 3 tablespoons) granulated sugar, divided
  • 1 teaspoon packed lemon zest, plus 2 teaspoons (10 ml) fresh juice, divided (from 1 medium lemon)
  • 1/4 cup (60 ml) neutral oil such as canola or vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon almond extract, optional
  • 3 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 300 g all-purpose flour (10 1/2 ounces; 2 1/3 cups)
  • 1 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt; for table salt, use half as much by volume
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 cup (240 ml) sour cream, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml) whole milk, at room temperature
  • 60 g (2 ounces) cream cheese, softened
  • 2 cups (475 ml) heavy whipping cream
  • 85 g (3 ounces) fresh strawberries, trimmed and thinly sliced (about 1/2 cup sliced)

    • Special Equipment



  • Hand-held electric mixer or stand mixer with whisk attachment, 9-by-13-inch baking pan, wooden spoon with a handle about 3/8-inch wide


    • Make-Ahead and Storage


  • The cake can be prepared through step 6, covered, and refrigerated for up to 5 days. Bring to room temperature for 30 minutes and top with whipped cream before serving.

    The assembled cake can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.
